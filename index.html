Usain Bolt

The Greatest Sprinter Ever

Usain St Leo Bolt is a Jamaican retired sprinter, widely considered to be the greatest sprinter of all time. He holds the world record for the 100m, 200m and 4Ã—100m relay. Here is look-back to a remarkable career:

  • 1986

    Born in Sherwood Content, Jamaica.

  • 2002

    Gold in the 200m dash and two silvers in the 4x100m and 4x400m at the Junior World Championships.

  • 2003

    Gold medal in the 200m at the World Youth Championships.

  • 2008

    Bolt sets world record time of 9.72s in the 100m at the Reebok Grand Prix, New York.
    Later that year at the Beijing Olympics, Bolt wins a triple in 100m, 200m and 4x100m setting world record in all: 9.69s in the 100m, 19.30s in the 200m and 37.10s in the 4x100m.

  • 2009

    Bolt sets a world record time of 9.58s for the 100m race at the Berlin 2009 World Championships. He also wins gold in the 200m and 4x100m.

  • 2012

    Triple gold in 100m, 200m and 4x100m at the London 2012 Olympic games.

  • 2016

    Triple gold in 100m, 200m and 4x100m at the Rio Olympic games.
